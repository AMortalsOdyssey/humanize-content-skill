# Medium Rules: Image

Use for image prompts, poster concepts, Xiaohongshu cards, thumbnails, infographics, and visual direction.

## Principle

Images should make the topic inspectable. Do not ask for a vague "futuristic AI scene" when the content is about a concrete workflow. Choose a primary object or moment that carries the idea.

## Prompt Ingredients

Include:

- Primary subject: one visible object, person, workspace, interface, or scene.
- Action or state: what is happening now.
- Evidence detail: files, checklists, logs, screenshots, sticky notes, cables, windows, annotations, or other inspectable artifacts.
- Mood: restrained, tense, calm, editorial, documentary, playful, etc.
- Composition: close-up, overhead, split-screen, grid, margin notes, or poster layout.
- Text treatment: exact title if needed; otherwise leave text minimal.

## Xiaohongshu Cards

- One idea per card.
- Big title should name the situation, not summarize all advice.
- Put proof or mechanism in small annotations.
- Avoid filling the image with abstract buzzwords.
- Prefer "失败现场", "改法", "验证方式", "边界" as card logic.

## Anti-AI Visual Checks

- Does the image have a concrete object that belongs to the topic?
- Could a viewer infer the work being done without reading a long caption?
- Is the visual too glossy for a practical note?
- Is the text short enough to remain readable on a phone?
- Are private paths, internal URLs, accounts, and secrets removed?

# Medium Rules: Text

Use for blog posts, tutorials, docs, product copy, Xiaohongshu notes, newsletters, and long-form social posts.

## Default Structures

Tutorial:

1. What changed or broke.
2. Why the naive approach fails.
3. The operating model.
4. Steps with artifacts.
5. How to verify.
6. Tradeoffs and where not to use it.

Xiaohongshu note:

1. Hook from a real mistake or tension.
2. Short concrete failure scene.
3. The corrected mechanism.
4. A few rules readers can apply.
5. Personal judgment or boundary.

Product or feature explanation:

1. Who had the problem.
2. The old workflow.
3. What the feature changes.
4. Required permissions, data, or setup.
5. Example use case.
6. What to check before relying on it.

## Human Voice Rules

- Keep one clear point per section.
- Use concrete nouns before abstract nouns.
- Prefer "我后来改成..." over "建议采用最佳实践..." when writing from lived experience.
- Use some short sentences. Do not polish every edge.
- Keep emojis sparse and functional for Xiaohongshu; remove them for docs and essays.
- Preserve the user's natural vocabulary when it is distinctive.

## Rewrite Checks

Before delivering, ask:

- Does the piece contain a real failure or constraint?
- Can the reader picture the workflow?
- Is there proof, not just confidence?
- Did the ending give a usable next step?
- Did any sentence sound like a generic motivational poster?
